Renewable Energy

Highway Divider Windmills

Maximizing infrastructure efficiency by harvesting airflow from Fast-Moving Traffic.

Concept Overview

This project explores the optimization of windmill placement by situating them in highway dividers. This innovative approach presents a new method for harvesting clean electricity by leveraging existing infrastructure. Windmills here can harness the consistent airflow generated by traffic combined with natural wind currents.

Land Optimization

Uses dead space in dividers, requiring no extra land.

Cost Reduction

Shared infrastructure for lower installation costs.

Grid Resilience

Decentralized generation reduces transmission losses.

Technological Specifications

Vertical Axis (VAWT)

Best for turbulent urban wind. Omnidirectional harvesting means it works regardless of wind or traffic direction.

Darrieus Rotor

"Eggbeater" shape offers high efficiency at high rotation speeds, making it ideal for the fast airflow conditions of a highway.

Hybrid Systems Integration

Can be integrated with top-mounted solar panels to generate power even on calm days with low traffic volume.
